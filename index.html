Four explainable signals of engineering impact, each traceable to specific PRs:

  • Review influence — substantive reviews delivered
  • Unblock speed — hours to first substantive review
  • Collaboration reach — distinct PR authors helped
  • Breadth — distinct subsystems touched

Signals to investigate, not a leaderboard. Full definitions and caveats in the methodology below.

Methodology

What each dimension measures, and how it can mislead. Read before reading the rankings.

Review influence

Count of substantive reviews delivered — i.e. requested changes, contained inline comments, or had a body ≥80 characters. LGTM drive-bys are excluded.

Watch forverbose-but-shallow reviews, or one hyperactive reviewer in a single area.

Unblock speed

Among PRs ≥20 LOC where this person gave the first substantive review, the median hours from PR open to that review. Lower is better; inverted so higher bar = faster.

Watch forPR-queue camping and timezone effects. Trust the absolute hours, not the percentile.

Collaboration reach

Number of distinct other contributors whose PRs this person substantively reviewed. Hardest of the four to game — credits cross-team helpers.

Watch fordrive-by reviewing many small PRs at large scale.

Breadth

Distinct top-level directories touched in merged PRs in the window. Excludes lockfiles, snapshots, generated dirs, migrations, CHANGELOGs, and root-level files.

Watch formechanical refactors that span the tree. Combine with review influence for substance.

What this dashboard can't see: design docs, Slack threads, mentorship, incident response, on-call work, customer conversations, off-hours debugging, and almost everything that happens before a PR is opened. GitHub data is a narrow slice of engineering impact — a starting point for a manager's investigation, never a substitute for one. Composite ranking is the unweighted mean of available dimension percentiles; contributors active in fewer than 2 dimensions are excluded.
